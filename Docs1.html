The Internet is a network of networks that consists of millions of computers spread across the globe. Its invention has replaced the old saying "the world is too small" with "global village." It has immensely transformed our lives. It has got numerous advantages but it also poses many threats, which could prove dangerous for us, if ignored. So, it is necessary to follow computer ethics and observe some safety measures while using Internet services.

CYBER ETHICS

The word ethics refers to moral standards and principles that govern the conduct and behavior of an individual. Computer ethics is a set of moral behavior that must be practiced while using a computer connected with the internet.

Some cyber ethics are listed below.
  • Never steal anyone's data or information.
  • Be respectful and avoid sarcasm.
  • Obey copyright laws.
  • Don't pose plagiarism.
  • Respect others' privacy.
  • Don't reveal sensitive or confidential information online.
  • Use appropriate language.
  • Don't indulge in spamming.
  • Be tolerant and careful
  • Remain aware and alert about various cyberattacks.

CYBER CRIMES

Cybercrimes refer to activities that violate cyberethics. In other words, an unethical or illegal activity that involves a computer and a network is called a cybercrime. It is carried out by an individual, a group of people, or an organization. Cybercrimes are punishable under law.

THERE ARE THREE TYPES OF CYBERCRIMES THAT ARE PERFORMED IN SOCIETY.

Cybercrimes against an individual

The offense committed against an individual with an intent to harm the reputation of the victim or cause physical or mental harm using the Internet is considered a crime.

Cybercrimes against a property

Cybercrime against a property refers to the crime that entails stealing someone's inatngible property using a computer and the Internet.

Cybercrimes against an organisation

Cybercrimes against an organization refers to the cybercrimes committed to create fear, to gain monetary benefits or to steal personal data and information of an organisation and society.
